Computer Graphics Quiz - Homogeneous Coordinates & Perspective

1. In one-point perspective, which lines converge to the vanishing point?

2. What is the homogeneous coordinate representation of a 3D point (1, 2, 3)?

3. How is a vector represented in homogeneous coordinates?

4. Why is matrix composition important for transformations?

5. What is foreshortening in perspective projection?

6. In homogeneous coordinates, (2,4,6,2) represents the same point as (1,2,3,1).


7. The number of vanishing points in perspective projection is limited to three.


8. Match each perspective type with its characteristic:

One-point perspective
Two-point perspective
Three-point perspective

9. Match each transformation with its homogeneous matrix characteristic:

Translation
Rotation

10. What happens to parallel lines in perspective projection that are NOT perpendicular to the view direction?

Answer Key:

Multiple Choice Questions:

1. b - Lines parallel to the view direction converge to the vanishing point

2. b - (1, 2, 3, 1) is the homogeneous coordinate representation

3. b - Vectors are represented as (x, y, z, 0) in homogeneous coordinates

4. b - Matrix composition reduces the number of operations needed

5. b - Foreshortening means evenly spaced points appear closer together at greater distances

10. b - They intersect at a vanishing point

True/False Questions:

6. True - Homogeneous coordinates are equivalent up to scale

7. False - More vanishing points are possible with misaligned objects

Matching Questions:

8. One-point → One set of parallel lines converges

8. Two-point → Two sets of parallel lines converge

8. Three-point → Three sets of parallel lines converge

9. Translation → Uses the rightmost column

9. Rotation → Affects only the 3x3 upper left

Explanations:

1. In one-point perspective, only lines parallel to the view direction converge to the vanishing point.

2. Homogeneous coordinates add a fourth coordinate (w), typically 1 for points.

3. Vectors use w=0 to indicate they have no position, only direction.

4. Composing transformations into a single matrix reduces computational cost.

5. Foreshortening is a key aspect of perspective projection that affects depth perception.

6. Homogeneous coordinates represent the same point when scaled proportionally.

7. Additional vanishing points can occur with complex arrangements of objects.

8. Each type of perspective is characterized by the number of parallel line sets that converge.

9. Different transformations affect different parts of the homogeneous matrix.

10. This is a fundamental principle of perspective projection.
