Answer Key:

Multiple Choice Questions:

1. b - Hue, Saturation, Value

2. d - (0,0,0) No reflection

3. b - Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, Key (Black)

4. c - CRT

5. b - The set of colors it can display

9. b - Blue LED

10. b - No need for raster scanning

True/False Questions:

6. False - Additive and subtractive color mixing work differently

7. False - CCDs store charge and then transfer it to memory

Matching Questions:

8. RGB → Digital displays

8. CMYK → Printing

8. HSV → Color wheel

Explanations:

1. HSV represents color in terms of Hue (color), Saturation (intensity), and Value (brightness).

2. Red light on cyan surface results in no reflection as cyan absorbs red.

3. CMYK is the standard model used in color printing.

4. CRTs require high refresh rates due to phosphor decay.

5. Gamut refers to the complete subset of colors that can be accurately represented.

6. Color mixing works differently for light (additive) vs. pigments (subtractive).

7. CCDs store charge before converting to digital signals.

8. Each color model is optimized for specific applications.

9. The development of efficient blue LEDs was a crucial breakthrough.

10. LCD technology doesn't require electron beam scanning.
