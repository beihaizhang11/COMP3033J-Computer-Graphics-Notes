Answer Key:

Multiple Choice Questions:

1. b - A pixel in a texture image

2. b - 0 to 1

3. a - GL_NEAREST

4. b - Surface parameters

5. b - Texture either clamps or repeats based on settings

9. a - glTexCoord2f()

10. b - To combine texture colors with lighting calculations

True/False Questions:

6. True - Bilinear interpolation uses 4 nearest texels

7. False - The texture matrix is identity by default

Matching Questions:

8. Scaling → Adjusts brightness by multiplication

8. Clamping → Ensures values stay in range

8. Rasterization → Converts to pixel format

Explanations:

1. A texel is the fundamental unit of a texture image, similar to a pixel.

2. OpenGL uses normalized coordinates from 0 to 1 for texture mapping.

3. GL_NEAREST is better for geometric textures as it preserves sharp edges.

4. (s,t) coordinates are surface parameters that map to texture coordinates.

5. Texture coordinates outside [0,1] can be handled by clamping or repeating.

6. Bilinear interpolation uses a weighted average of the 4 nearest texels.

7. The texture matrix starts as identity until modified.

8. Each texture operation serves a specific purpose in texture processing.

9. glTexCoord2f() is the standard function for specifying texture coordinates.

10. Texture modulation combines texture colors with lighting calculations.
