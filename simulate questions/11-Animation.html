Answer Key:

Multiple Choice Questions:

1. b - Double buffering prevents screen tearing and visual artifacts

2. b - The pelvis serves as the master bone in the hierarchy

3. b - A keyframe is an important frame with explicitly specified joint angles

4. b - 24 fps is typically the minimum for smooth animation

5. b - Inverse Kinematics calculates joint angles for desired end positions

9. b - Scene graphs provide hierarchical scene description

10. b - A bone is a separately transformed piece with its own coordinate system

True/False Questions:

6. False - Motion capture data needs modification for different character sizes

7. True - glPushMatrix() and glPopMatrix() must be balanced

Matching Questions:

8. Motion Capture → Measures real movement

8. Physical Simulation → Relies on physics equations

8. Keyframe Animation → Uses important frames

Explanations:

1. Double buffering prevents visual artifacts by showing completed frames only.

2. The pelvis is the root of the transformation hierarchy in character animation.

3. Keyframes are explicitly defined poses from which in-between frames are interpolated.

4. 24 fps is the traditional minimum for smooth motion perception.

5. IK solves for joint angles to achieve desired end effector positions.

6. Motion capture data needs scaling and adjustment for different character sizes.

7. Matrix stack operations must be balanced to maintain proper transformations.

8. Each animation technique has distinct characteristics and use cases.

9. Scene graphs organize hierarchical relationships in a scene.

10. Bones are transformation units in character animation, not necessarily anatomical.
